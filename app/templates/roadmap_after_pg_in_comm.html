Career Options After Post-Graduation in Commerce

Master of Commerce graduates can access a pool of opportunities in various business domains and management industries. With a degree in hand, you can go for the following job positions - Company Secretary, Auditor, Business Analyst, Consultant, Tax Accountant, Stock Broker, Finance Officer, Sales Analyst, and Economist. Moreover, you have significant positions to appear in government exams applying for public sector jobs. Here are some reliable career options after M. Com:

1.Accounts/Finance Manager

Financial Institutions and Organizational Banks, including Yes Bank or Canara Bank, offer job roles like Money Manager, Cashier, Money Teller, Assistant Accountant, etc. You can enroll for relative courses that help you pursue the field of your interest. Aurora degree and PG college give you comprehensive courses to help you out with successive guides. The paid role varies between INR 3- 4 LPA based on the roles.

2.Chartered Financial Analyst [CFA]

CFA, an acronym for the Chartered Financial Analyst job role, opens a doorway into the investment and finance industry. The position is all about financial analysis, variant bonds and derivatives, kinds of portfolios and investment management. You need to pass through the 3 tiers to apply for CFA. Goldman Sachs, Royal Bank of Canada, and JP Morgan are some top global financial companies offering an average salary of Rs. 6.6 LPA approximately.

3.Financial Risk Manager [FRM]

Risk Management is a flourishing industry in the current vigorous financial market. The financial risk manager position is a great career option after post-graduation in commerce if you are ready to undergo a 9-month agenda promoted by the GARP. Being an FRM, you should possess the ability to recognize and examine potential financial threats. You can also explore the services like financial services, credit and market risk, trading management, sales and marketing. The average pay scale for FRM professionals varies between 10 to 18 LPA

4.Certified Financial Planner [CFP]

CFP-certified candidates need to undergo a short-term course to examine avenues in finance and insurance companies. This is yet another trend domain, and students actively participate in the course. Some of the best courses offered by Aurora degree and PG college are worth opting for. You need to be good at the following parameters like educational performance, work experience demonstration, along with professional ethics. While going with this career option, you are free to explore the areas of insurance decisions, tax planning, estate planning, retirement, investment management, and many more. The average pay scale of CFP is approximately Rs. 3.9 LPA.

5.Certificate in Investment Banking [CIB]

CIB is an acronym for Certificate in Investment Banking. If you want to raise capital for firms, you can apply to this sector - a 6-month program to sell equity and issue debt. Now, it is a globally known course to check whether you can employ systematic or a means of strategy. The candidate should be able to develop the quotation of listed/unlisted organizations. You get access to coursework like equity research, asset management themes, h corporate finance strategy, hedge funds, and private wealth management. The average pay scale for an investment banking career is around INR 9.6 lakh approximately.

6.Indian Administration Services

A postgraduate candidate in commerce can feasibly enroll for an IAS position by passing a UPSC or other state public service assessment. You can go for jobs in Defence, Railways, PSU, Police, and Revenue departments incorporated by governmental services. The starting pay scale salary for this particular position is INR 6 LPA.

7.Teaching & Research

If you want to explore your interest in academic disciplines and business, you can enter the research industry. A doctorate or a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) is now in majors like Operations Research, Finance, Statistics, Organizational Behaviour, HRM [Human Resource Management], etc. Additionally, there are numerous national and international fellowships to evince high research aptitude in the field of Economics, Commerce, as well as Management. You can also look out to become a university lecturer, despite no job limitations.

Final Thoughts

In this article, we have sorted a list of promising career options after post-graduation in Commerce. We hope you get a precise answer to “What can you pursue after post-graduation?”. To go for the correct choice, you need to analyze the difficulty level, the time required, and the price of the program before deciding on career options. If you go for any of the above options, you tend to land a good job, so gather some more research and start over your journey in the professional world. Make sure you follow the path you desire!
